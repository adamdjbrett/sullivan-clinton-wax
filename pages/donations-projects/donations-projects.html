Sullivanclinton.com was born this year, the 225th Anniversary year of the Sullivan-Clinton Campaign. The Project is an ongoing collaboration of educators and artists, designers and you, our visitors. With your help, it will continue far beyond this year as we work to advance democratic values and mutual respect.

The Sullivan/Clinton Campaign is one of the least-known, but most important events in our history. As you read, New York is hosting a few one-time commemorations that will attract tourist dollars. These help to keep its memory alive, but they're nowhere near enough. Because Sullivan/Clinton has been well "off the radar," we ask your help in educating visitors, teachers and students about its role in American history and its enduring impact on the Iroquois/Haudenosaunee people.

While the Project's main focus is Dispossession, related topics include Sullivan/Clinton's impact on America's Westward Expansion; how it cleared the way for the Erie Canal; and how it boosted New York City's rise to world-class prominence. And, last but not least, we'll show its continuing relevance today.

As style counts, all this will be done with a contemporary look-and-feel and with engaging multimedia and interactive approaches.

THE WEBSITE

Your Donation supports the continuous evolution of sullivanclinton.com. It takes fresh approaches to the Sullivan-Clinton Campaign and shows its ongoing relevance today. Gallery and Map pages are updated often. So too the Audio/Visual page where exciting artists worldwide can share their talent and vision. A major feature is the blogs where viewers post comments and add content. And twin Search Engines give teachers, students, scholars and history buffs a great research tool on texts and images for tonight's homework or their continuing education.

THE BOOK: FIELDS OF FIRE

Your Donation or Purchase helps finance the companion book, Fields of Fire: The Sullivan-Clinton Campaign, Then & Now. It presents Sullivan/Clinton in fresh ways with links to today's events. It has a great guest interview with Seneca spokesperson, Peter Jemison, and a powerful essay on its forgotten aftermath, the "Winter of Hunger." It offers "Who's Who" sketches of key personalities and a "Where and When" chronology of most events. Also location and content of many NY & Pennsylvania road markers. All this plus current & historical photos. It's available in August, so reserve your copy now!

THE SULLIVAN-CLINTON MAPSET

Your Donation or Purchase helps develop a beautiful CD-Rom MapSet (version 1.0). It's an interactive journey through the Sullivan/Clinton saga. Click through its scenes for views of: the original Six Nations; their break-up in the American Revolution; the main routes and flashpoints of the military campaign; the tragic aftermath in Ft. Niagara; the land's division into Purchases; the advent of canals, railroads and highways; and places where the Iroquois live today. See sample views now on the Map page. With 5 more to come, the MapSet is for PCs at home, libraries and classrooms. It's available in August, 2004, so reserve your copy now!

EDUCATIONAL OUTREACH

Your Donation helps develop fresh ideas and materials for including the Sullivan-Clinton Campaign in lesson plans, school curricula and homework assignments, for high schools and colleges. These aim to make Sullivan/Clinton and its related themes come alive for teachers, students and all curious learners of 15 years and over.
